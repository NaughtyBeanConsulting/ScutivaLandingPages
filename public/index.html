Scutiva helps you generate SBOMs and find CVEs across your Linux environments.

Scutiva is a self-hosted security dashboard focused on software inventory and vulnerability visibility. It helps teams understand what is installed, identify known CVEs, and review findings in one place without relying on a hosted platform.

What Scutiva does

Install it on your own infrastructure and simplify CVE review.

Scutiva gives you one place to generate SBOMs, scan packages for known vulnerabilities, and turn raw scanner output into a clearer remediation workflow. Instead of piecing together several tools by hand, you get a more usable view of what needs attention.

How it works

Install Scutiva, scan your systems, and review CVEs with more context.

Scutiva is designed to be straightforward to adopt. You deploy it in your own environment, connect the Linux systems you care about, run SBOM and vulnerability scans, and review the results from a single interface.

  • Connect systems you want to scan
  • Generate software inventory and SBOM data
  • Review CVE findings in one place
  • Keep deployment and data under your control

What you get

Practical visibility into software inventory and vulnerability exposure.

Area 1

SBOM and package inventory

Scutiva helps you understand what packages and components are installed so you have a clearer software inventory to work from.

  • Builds a clearer picture of installed software
  • Helps teams understand component and package coverage
  • Creates a stronger foundation for CVE review
Area 2

CVE visibility

Scutiva helps you see where installed packages and application dependencies may be exposing you to known vulnerabilities.

  • Surfaces vulnerable packages and components
  • Helps teams focus remediation on known issues
  • Makes vulnerability review easier to understand and act on

Why it matters

Scutiva gives you a simpler way to stay on top of software exposure.

Many teams need better visibility into what software is installed and which known CVEs may affect them, but do not want to hand over infrastructure data to a hosted vendor. Scutiva gives those teams a practical, open-source option they can install and operate themselves.
